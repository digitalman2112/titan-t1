## Setup

1. Remove any <span class="ref-accessory">exercise handles</span> or attachments from the smith carriage.
2. Insert <span class="ref-cable">crossover cable</span> A1-L into hole **5**, **6**, or **7-L** depending on the placement of the first pulley. Insert A1-R into the corresponding hole on the right side according to preference.
3. Using a clip, attach D-grip handles to cables C9-L and C9-R, if they are not already attached.
4. Disconnect cable C9-L from C9-L storage and C9-R from C9-R storage. Make sure the cables are not twisted.
5. Select the desired weight.
6. If the weight of the smith carriage is too heavy, insert <span class="ref-cable">counter-balance cable</span> <span class="ref-cable">C2</span> into hole **18** and add the desired counter weight. Do not exceed your known performance capabilities.

## Cable Cross Over

1. Firmly grasp the D-grip handles in each hand.
2. Assume a comfortable seated position with the back resting against the backrest.
3. Slide the bench to position **5** (adjust to preference) and lock into place. Adjust the backrest and seat to the desired incline position and lock into place using the pull pins underneath.
4. Plant the feet firmly on the ground or use the provided footrest.
5. Perform the desired exercise (incline/decline/chest press, cable pec fly, or overhead triceps) by lifting and lowering the weight in a slow, controlled manner, pausing briefly in the fully contracted position.
6. For a cable crossover: in a slow, controlled manner, squeeze the grips together in front of the chest with the arms slightly bent. Upon completion, gently lower the weight to the original starting position.

## After Completing

1. If the counter-balance was used, remove any weight from the weight post, then remove <span class="ref-cable">C2</span> from hole **18** and return to the storage hole.
2. Attach cable C9-R to C9-R storage and cable C9-L to C9-L storage.
3. Remove crossover cables A1-L and A1-R and insert into holes **28-L** and **28-R** located on the cable crossover arms.

## Precautions

- Be sure that all cables and pulleys are properly and securely attached.
