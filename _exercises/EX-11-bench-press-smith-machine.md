## Setup

1. Insert <span class="ref-accessory">exercise handle</span> <span class="ref-accessory">S1-R</span> into hole **11-R** and handle <span class="ref-accessory">S1-L</span> into hole **11-L** on the smith carriage. Lift the smith carriage and lock onto hooks <span class="ref-hook">H-5</span> or <span class="ref-hook">H-6</span>.
2. If the weight of the smith carriage without added weight is too heavy, insert <span class="ref-cable">counter-balance cable</span> <span class="ref-cable">C2</span> into hole **18** and add the desired counter weight.
3. Be sure that the weights are evenly distributed on each side of the machine.
4. Adjust the bench to position **9** (adjust to preference) and be sure that it is locked into place.
5. Enter the machine carefully. Assume a comfortable reclining position with your back resting firmly against the back of the seat and your feet on the provided footrest.

## Bench Press

1. Unlock both <span class="ref-accessory">exercise handles</span> simultaneously when beginning the exercise.
2. In a slow and controlled manner, lift weight by <span class="ref-accessory">exercise handles</span>, pause briefly in the near fully extended position, and return to the starting position.
3. When finished, lock both <span class="ref-accessory">handles</span> securely onto the <span class="ref-hook">hooks</span>.

## After Completing

1. If a counter-balance was used, remove any weight from the weight post, then remove <span class="ref-cable">cable</span> <span class="ref-cable">C2</span> from hole **18** and return it to its storage hole.
2. Unlock the <span class="ref-accessory">exercise handles</span> from the <span class="ref-hook">hooks</span> and lower the smith carriage so it rests on the springs.
3. Remove <span class="ref-accessory">exercise handles</span> <span class="ref-accessory">S1-R</span> and <span class="ref-accessory">S1-L</span>.

## Precautions

- Always ensure **both** <span class="ref-accessory">handles</span> are securely locked before starting and when finishing. Never secure the smith carriage by locking only one side.
